**ADVERSARIAL REVIEW**

The distillation claims balance by construction ("four conflicting witness positions") and asserts it "deliberately give[s] the industry and civil-liberties witnesses their strongest statements, not caricatures." It fails on every metric. It flattens the hearing into a pro-regulation narrative that pre-loads de-amplify.com’s thesis, co-opts Ruane’s mechanism language while erasing her remedy conflict, and minimizes Lekas and Ruane.

**1. BALANCE PRESERVED (inverted wedge risk)**  
The distillation does not give Lekas or Ruane their strongest arguments. It subordinates them to a "how to build a working one" frame.

Evidence:  
- Distillation states: "the disagreement is not about what the engine is; it is about which wrench to use on it."  
- Ruane’s actual testimony: "blocking or restricting minors’ access to social media or 'harmful content' does not address the larger issues underlying the reported increase in youth mental health problems or the surveillance capitalism business model... That requires passing privacy legislation."  
- Lekas: "This scrutiny forces Congress toward less restrictive means, such as regulating design or privacy conduct, rather than content." The distillation buries the force of this constitutional warning under "industry: yes to safety law, calibrated."

The four-way split is reduced to a single policy debate whose correct answer is design mandates.

**2. QUOTE FIDELITY & FAIR REPRESENTATION**  
Quotes are often verbatim but stripped of context that shows opposition.

- Ruane is quoted naming "the surveillance capitalism business model" and warning on age verification ("create significant privacy risks").  
  Buried: her explicit opposition to the bills’ core mechanisms ("does not address the larger issues"), her call for data-minimization privacy law as the root fix, her First Amendment arguments for minors’ speech rights, and her rejection of preemption that would block stronger state laws.  
- Lekas is quoted supporting "laws that improve youth privacy and safety" while warning on unintended consequences.  
  Buried: his detailed analysis that content-based "harm" duties transform into strict-scrutiny violations (NetChoice v. Bonta), his view that Paxton logic "is unlikely to extend to general-purpose social media platforms," and his list of pro-social third-party data uses that broad mandates would disrupt.

**3. MECHANISM-NOT-TRIBE vs FALSE CONVERGENCE**  
The distillation co-opts Ruane’s mechanism language while dropping that her remedy directly conflicts.

Evidence:  
- Distillation: "even Ruane named the same mechanism the other hearings did... The disagreement is not about what the engine is."  
- Ruane (full context): names the model only to reject the proposed remedies as bandaids that leave it untouched. She states the durable fix is "comprehensive consumer data privacy legislation that meaningfully realigns incentives for the business models" via data minimization—not design or content mandates. The distillation treats privacy law as one optional "wrench" rather than a conflicting alternative that renders KOSA-style duties unnecessary and unconstitutional.

**4. AUDIENCE / RECEPTION**  
An industry or civil-liberties reader would see a pro-regulation brief, not a fair map.

- Ruane would not recognize her position as fairly represented; the distillation converts her root-cause privacy argument into evidence that "the mechanism is agreed."  
- Lekas would see his constitutional and preemption warnings reduced to mild calibration advice.  
- The framing ("the brake is broken. This one is the debate over how to build a working one") signals the site’s conclusion before any witness speaks.

**5. MANUFACTURED NEUTRALITY / STEALTH-ADVOCACY**  
The framing pre-loads the conclusion.

Specific lines:  
- "The other three hearings establish that the brake is broken. This one is the debate over how to build a working one, and what breaks if you build it wrong."  
- "The mechanism is agreed, even across the aisle of this debate."  
These sentences import the site’s thesis from the other (one-sided) distillations and treat Ruane’s and Lekas’s objections as technical implementation details rather than fundamental rejections of the remedy class.
