## Findings (most severe first)

### F1. A verbatim-fidelity violation exists in the ledger, and the frontmatter's "every quote checked" claim is therefore false — SEVERE (violates a Done criterion)

**(a) Claim:** The ledger silently altered punctuation inside a quoted verbatim span.
**(b) Evidence:** Pilot ledger ("The mechanism" section): `"in reality, kind of a placebo, a safety feature in name only to placate the press and regulators"`. Transcript (line 622–624): `"in reality, kind of a placebo--a safety feature in name only to placate the press and regulators"`. Scripted check: the ledger's comma version is **MISSING** from the transcript; the `--` version is FOUND. The doc's sources block asserts "Every verbatim quote below was checked as an exact substring of this transcript," and the plan's status line claims "100% verbatim substrings."
**(c) Why it matters:** This is exactly the failure class the plan calls load-bearing and non-negotiable (Method §2, Done criterion 2). Meaning isn't changed here, but the discipline claim is now overstated, and the root cause is systematic: the no-em-dash house style collided with verbatim quoting, and the drafter resolved it by normalizing *inside* quotation marks. It also reveals the substring check ran only on the `## Quotes` section, not on quoted spans in the ledger/summary/brake sections. Note the GPO plain text uses `--` (not U+2014), so quoting it verbatim passes an em-dash scan anyway — the "fix" was unnecessary.
**(d) Fix:** Restore `placebo--a safety feature` verbatim (or split into two quoted substrings around unquoted connective, as the doc already does correctly for "measure our help ... was not adopted"). Add an explicit rule to the plan: **verbatim quotes are exempt from house style; GPO's `--` is quoted as-is.** Make the substring check cover every quoted span anywhere in the doc.

### F2. The pilot quietly redefined the evidence tiers relative to the plan, and the plan was not amended — SEVERE (scaling hazard)

**(a) Claim:** Plan and pilot use the same tier names with different semantics.
**(b) Evidence:** Plan Method §4: ESTABLISHED = "verbatim in the official Congress.gov transcript PDF or official written-testimony PDF"; OBSERVED = "carried only by a cleaned secondary transcript... or news coverage." Pilot tier_key: ESTABLISHED = "the statement was made on the record at this hearing (a fact about what was SAID)"; OBSERVED = "carried by a document read into the record or by outside reporting cited at the hearing."
**(c) Why it matters:** Under the plan's definition, the WSJ passage Hawley read aloud IS verbatim in the official transcript, hence ESTABLISHED; under the pilot's it is OBSERVED. The pilot's semantics are genuinely better (they separate "the hearing witnessed this being said" from "a second-hand document was read into the record"), but two docs written under the two keys will tier identical material differently. The plan's cleaned-transcript OBSERVED category and its mandatory "cleaned-transcript wording — verify against official record" flag vanished from the pilot's tier_key entirely. Also, the pilot's `[lawmaker characterization]` flag is **defined but used zero times** (count: 1, the definition itself) — while lawmaker assertions that would need it (Blumenthal's "2 percent of the time" action rate, the 7-percent suicide-content stat) were simply omitted instead of carried-with-flag.
**(d) Fix:** Amend the plan's Method §4 to adopt the pilot's tier semantics explicitly (keep the cleaned-transcript rule as a separate provenance flag), and either exercise `[lawmaker characterization]` on real claims or delete it before the CEO doc inherits a dead flag.

### F3. The pilot's own hard-won insights are noted in the findings appendix but NOT wired into the plan's normative workflow — SEVERE (this is the exact question the pilot was supposed to answer before scaling)

**(a) Claim:** The two-pass adversarial wedge check and the audit-the-removed-set discipline exist only in the "Execution and findings" narrative, not in Method / gate C / Output format.
**(b) Evidence:** Method gate C(c) still reads "the quote-set passes the Both-Sides swap + speaker-balance check"; Execution step 4 still specifies a single "non-Claude review lens." The insights section says the check "should be two-pass" and "Add to the workflow: after trimming, audit the removed set" — but nothing in the normative sections was edited. Likewise the Output format template lacks `tier_key`, `structural_villain`, `sycophancy_audit`, and the `## Brake-integrity relevance` section, all of which the pilot added.
**(c) Why it matters:** The normative spec is what a fresh session or sub-agent executes for the Jan 2024 hearing. Insights buried in an appendix are precisely what gets skipped after compaction — the pilot proved the cooperative pass rubber-stamps (r1: "No fixes required") and only the adversarial pass found the exclusion asymmetry. Scaling without wiring that in reproduces the rubber-stamp on the highest-wedge-risk hearing.
**(d) Fix:** Edit Method §B and gate C to mandate: two-pass (cooperative + adversarial) external check, removed-set skew audit, and the logged `sycophancy_audit` block as a required frontmatter field (the news-essay v2 precedent: no block → not finalized). Update the Output format template to match what the pilot actually ships.

### F4. The exclusion-asymmetry disclosure is itself asymmetric: Democratic theater lines were also cut but never named — MODERATE-SEVERE (the doc over-concedes, and the external audit was taken at face value)

**(a) Claim:** The coverage note names only Republican exclusions, but the transcript contains excluded Democratic dunk lines too; the r2 adversarial finding ("No comparable Democratic 'theater' lines are flagged or dropped") is factually wrong and was adopted without verification.
**(b) Evidence from the transcript, all excluded and none disclosed:** Blumenthal: "pat you on the head and, in effect, tell you to go away, be a good boy" (line 704–705); "was all a charade, a mockery" (746); "Big Tech is the next Big Tobacco. **(c) Why it matters:** The doc amended its coverage note to concede an R-skew that is partly an artifact of which exclusions got *named*. A hostile reader who checks the transcript will find the disclosure itself fails the symmetry test it exists to demonstrate. Secondary lesson: the external reviewer's claims also need substring-checking — r2 additionally asserts "Graham's brief Section 230 sunset remark" is in the kept set; no Graham quote exists in the quote-bank.
**(d) Fix:** Amend the disclosure to list the excluded D-side theater (charade/mockery, Big-Tobacco riffs, doctored-data zinger) alongside the R-side list; correct the record on what the kept set actually contains. Add to the workflow: verify the external auditor's factual assertions against the transcript before adopting its findings.

### F5. Mechanism omissions — three strong ones a fair reader would want (dimension 4)

Scanned the full transcript against the ledger/quote-bank. The strongest omissions, all pure mechanism, none theater:

1. **The ad-vs-child-DM feedback asymmetry** — stated twice: "You can take an ad and say that is sexually inappropriate. But there's no way for a child to do that when they get a message" (lines 858–862, Hawley Q&A; repeated at 1744–1748, Butler Q&A). This is arguably the single most concrete brake-integrity receipt in the hearing: the feedback mechanism *exists in the product* for commercial content and is withheld from children's DMs. Its omission while "algorithms are as good as their inputs" is kept means the doc kept the abstraction and dropped the receipt.
2. **The engagement-mechanism admissions inside the excluded Kennedy exchange** — Kennedy: "isn't it true that some forms of social media optimize for engagement?" Bejar: "Yes. I think using your term, they reward being an a-hole" (1189–1192); plus the daughter anecdote: "I will not delete it because I'm worried that that will mean that less people will see my posts" (1208–1211) — the product punishes self-protective behavior via reach. The coverage note discarded the whole exchange as comic theater, but a mechanism admission (engagement optimization rewards harassment) and a felt-moment mechanism proof were inside it. This also exposes a **false dichotomy in the sycophancy_audit resolution** ("re-adding the trimmed partisan lines would reduce mechanism focus"): rescuing the mechanism kernels from the Kennedy exchange would *increase* mechanism focus AND partially repair the disclosed R-skew simultaneously.
3. **"prioritizing prevalence over harm" + the Chris Cox moment** — Bejar names the internal metric mechanism in one phrase: "prioritizing prevalence over harm, is something that Mark sets direction for" (1425–1426), the load-bearing internal counterpart of "creating its own homework"; and the Cox exchange: "The first person to do that was Chris Cox. And I found it heartbreaking because it meant that they knew and that they were not acting on it" (742–744) — the most concrete knew-and-didn't-act moment in the record. Honorable mentions: the 2-percent action-rate stat (275–276, needs the `[lawmaker characterization]` flag F2 says is unused), and "there are no goals to reduce unwanted sexual advances" (1312–1313).

**Fix:** Add these to the ledger/quote-bank before Gate 2 sign-off; they strengthen the doc's own thesis and cost nothing on the wedge axis.

### F6. Sources honesty: the appendix/addendum PDF is missing, and two listed sources are load-bearing for nothing — MODERATE

**(a) Claim:** The doc's most-leaned-on documents (the Oct 5 Zuckerberg email, the Oct 14 Mosseri email, the Massachusetts complaint) live in `CHRG-118shrg60432-add1.pdf`, which the transcript itself points to (lines 2310–2328) — and it is absent from the sources list. ### F8. Process gaps the plan does not yet cover for the Jan 2024 stress test — MODERATE (dimension 5 roll-up)

Beyond F3/F7: (1) **Adversarial-witness tiering** — the tier_key was built for a friendly single whistleblower; live CEO testimony needs a symmetric `[company-witness / interested-party]` flag for self-serving factual claims ("we spend $X on safety"), with report-don't-adjudicate discipline. (2) **Company-balance axis** — the villain-balance check is party-shaped; with five platforms, the quote-bank can cluster on Meta while Snap/Discord/X evidence exists. (3) **Confrontation set-pieces** — the famous moments (Zuckerberg's stand-and-apologize, "blood on your hands") are neither pure senator-dunk nor pure mechanism; the plan needs a pre-committed rule (keep an exchange only if the answer or refusal reveals mechanism — default settings, staffing, tool efficacy). (4) **Length vs the direct-distillation lesson** — insight 3 says direct-not-sub-agent, but a transcript several times longer strains one context; mandate chunked direct passes plus the committed verification script rather than re-deciding ad hoc. (5) **Verification tooling is uncommitted** — the "automated pass" that caught the drafter's slips exists nowhere retrievable; F1 proves it also had a coverage hole. (6) **Coverage-note quantification** — "roughly the opening statements and the ... exchanges" won't guard selection bias on a 60k-word record; require rough share estimates per topic.
